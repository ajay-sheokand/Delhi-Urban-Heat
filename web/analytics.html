Heat Vulnerability by Ward

Delhi's 11 districts are too large to show who is actually most exposed — each covers millions of people. This section re-runs the LST, NDVI and population inputs at ward resolution (290 wards, pre-2022 delimitation) to rank the areas where high surface heat, low vegetation and high population density overlap. Vulnerability score = the average of min-max normalized LST, inverse-normalized NDVI, and normalized population density per ward (0–100, higher = more vulnerable). Air temperature is intentionally not part of this score and stays district-level only (see the caveat above) — NASA POWER's ~50km grid and city-scale weather stations don't carry real information at ward scale, so adding them here would be false precision, not more signal. See the map's "Ward Boundaries" layer to click into any individual ward.

Informal Settlements: A Complementary Risk Layer

Delhi's official JJ (Jhuggi-Jhopri) clusters — squatter settlements on public land, mapped by DUSIB (Delhi Urban Shelter Improvement Board) — are a separate housing category from the general heat/vegetation/density signal the vulnerability score above is built from. We checked whether the two actually line up, expecting some overlap. They mostly don't: the correlation between vulnerability score and JJ-cluster household density across all 290 wards is —, and most of the top-ranked wards by vulnerability score (the dense, hot, green-poor Northeast Delhi belt) contain zero officially mapped JJ clusters. That's not a data error — Delhi's dense unplanned housing spans several distinct legal categories (JJ clusters, unauthorized colonies, urban villages), and DUSIB's list covers only one of them. The practical implication: a ward can score low here on satellite-visible heat and density while still containing residents facing real housing-specific risk (informal construction, no piped water or drainage, insecure tenure) that LST/NDVI/population data simply cannot see. Treat this layer as additional information about who is at risk, not as a check on whether the score above is "correct." See the map's "Informal Settlements" layer to click into individual clusters.
