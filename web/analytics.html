Heat Vulnerability by Ward

Delhi's 11 districts are too large to show who is actually most exposed — each covers millions of people. This section re-runs the LST, NDVI and population inputs at ward resolution (290 wards, pre-2022 delimitation) to rank the areas where high surface heat, low vegetation and high population density overlap. Vulnerability score = the average of min-max normalized LST, inverse-normalized NDVI, and normalized population density per ward (0–100, higher = more vulnerable). Air temperature is intentionally not part of this score and stays district-level only (see the caveat above) — NASA POWER's ~50km grid and city-scale weather stations don't carry real information at ward scale, so adding them here would be false precision, not more signal. See the map's "Ward Boundaries" layer to click into any individual ward.
